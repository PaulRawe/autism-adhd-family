üö® PROBLEM: Constant Out-of-Seat Behavior

What you see: Student gets up 15+ times per class period. Sharpens pencil, gets water, "needs" to talk to you, walks around the room.

What's really happening: ADHD brains need movement to stay alert. Sitting still depletes dopamine further. Their body is literally demanding movement.

‚úÖ IMMEDIATE SOLUTION: Legitimate Movement Opportunities

What to do right now:

  1. Give student a job that requires movement (paper distributor, errand runner)
  2. Allow "movement breaks" ‚Äì student can stand at desk or walk to designated spot
  3. Provide fidget tools for in-seat movement (therapy putty, stress ball)
  4. Schedule movement every 20 minutes (stretch break, quick walk to office)

Why this works: Provides the dopamine boost ADHD brain needs while keeping student on task

Result you'll see: Disruptive wandering decreases dramatically when legitimate movement is built in

üö® PROBLEM: Can't Start Tasks (Executive Function Paralysis)

What you see: Student stares at blank paper for 20 minutes. Knows the assignment. Has materials. Just won't start.

What's really happening: ADHD executive dysfunction = can't initiate tasks. It's not laziness ‚Äì their brain literally cannot sequence the steps to begin.

‚úÖ IMMEDIATE SOLUTION: Task Initiation Support

What to do right now:

  1. Break task into micro-steps: "Write your name. Done? Now write the date."
  2. Start together: "Let's do the first one together, then you try the next"
  3. Use timers: "Work for 5 minutes, then we check progress"
  4. Reduce choice paralysis: "Start with #1 or #3 ‚Äì you pick"

Why this works: Externalizes the executive function they can't access internally

Result you'll see: Student begins work consistently with minimal prompting
